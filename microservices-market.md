<p><p>Microservices are software architectural design in which a complex application is broken down into smaller, independent services that communicate with each other over a network. These services can be deployed in two main market types: on-premise and cloud-based. On-premise market involves hosting and managing the microservices within the organization's own infrastructure, providing more control and security. Cloud-based market, on the other hand, involves hosting the microservices on a third-party cloud platform, offering scalability, flexibility, and cost-effectiveness.</p></p>
