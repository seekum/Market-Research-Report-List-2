<p><p>Utility boats can be categorized into two main market types based on their propulsion system: Inboard Motor and Outboard Motor. Inboard Motor boats have the engine situated inside the hull, providing better weight distribution and performance. They are often preferred for larger vessels and commercial applications. On the other hand, Outboard Motor boats have the engine mounted on the transom, allowing for easier maintenance and maneuverability. They are commonly used for smaller recreational boats and fishing vessels. Both market types cater to diverse customer needs and preferences in the utility boat industry.</p></p>
