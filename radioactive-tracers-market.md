<p><p>Radioactive tracers are commonly used in medical imaging techniques such as Positron Emission Tomography (PET) and Single Photon Emission Computed Tomography (SPECT). PET tracers involve the injection of a radiolabeled substance into the body which emits positrons, allowing for detailed imaging of metabolic processes. On the other hand, SPECT tracers emit gamma rays to provide information about blood flow and tissue function. Both PET and SPECT markets are growing rapidly due to the increasing demand for advanced imaging technologies in healthcare.</p></p>
